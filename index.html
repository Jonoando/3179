Australian Wine Producers by State

Industry Insight

Victoria was the largest wine-producing state for a long time until South Australia overtook it in the late 2010s. New South Wales has consistently maintained 3rd spot. Despite year-to-year variations, the number of producers has remained relatively stable, suggesting a mature and well-established wine industry. Note: ACT and NSW were combined in this visualisation.

Source: Winetitles Australia

Per-Capita Alcohol Consumption Over Time

Consumption Trend

Alcohol consumption in Australia peaked in the mid-1970s at over 13 litres per person, driven largely by beer. Since then, overall consumption has gradually declined. However, wine consumption has shown a steady upward trend, highlighting a shift towards wine despite reduced total alcohol intake. Because wine is stronger than beer, even modest increases can contribute more to total alcohol intake.

Source: Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS)
