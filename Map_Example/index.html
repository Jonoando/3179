Industry Insight

Victoria was the largest wine producing state for a long time until South Australia overtook it from the late 2010's. New South Wales has consitently maintian 3rd spot. Despite year to year variations, the number of producers has remained relatively stable over recent years, showing that supply has most likely caught up with demand as well as a mature and well established wine industry. note: ACT and NSW were combined in this visualisation

Per-Capita Alcohol Consumption Over Time

Consumption Trend

Alcohol consumption in Australia peaked in the mid 1970s at over 13 litres per person, driven largely by beer. Since then, overall consumption has gradually declined. However, wine consumption has shown a steady upward trend, highlighting a shift in preferences toward wine despite reduced total alcohol intake.
